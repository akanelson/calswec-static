Curricula for Social Workers, Probation Officers, and Eligibility Workers

Warning message

You are currently visiting the archived CalSWEC website. To return to the new CalSWEC website at any time, go to https://calswec.berkeley.edu.

Here you’ll find curricula providing basic overview information and more in-depth information about Fostering Connections suitable for training social workers, probation officers, and eligibility workers.

  • After 18 Summit Facilitation Guide: The After 18 Summit Facilitation Guide and Materials are intended to assist in the planning and facilitation of an After 18 Summit.  The guide has been organized to provide an overview of the variety of elements of an After 18 Summit.  In addition, the guide provides all templates and planning materials needed for the coordination of a successful event from start to finish.
    • View a webinar presentation from Joni Pitcl (Prism Consulting) and Sevaughn Banks and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) which includes an overview of the Summit Facilitation Guide.
  • Implementation Guide for Supervisors: This implementation guide was developed for supervisors to help staff become familiar with the legislation and the nuances of the law. It provides background materials for review and reference, provides materials to introduce this bill to the supervisor's unit, and provides tools for implementation.
    • AB 12 Overview PowerPoint Presentation
    • View a webinar presentation from Sylvia Deporto (Assistant Director, Riverside County DPSS), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) providing AB12 Implementation steps for supervisors to use with their units, including training materials and tools for identifying AB12-eligible youth.
  • Supervisor Guide for Unit Meeting Training: This guide is intended to assist supervisors in providing brief (45-60 minute) training to staff in a unit meeting setting.  The guide includes five topics and provides sample agendas, talking points, handouts, suggested additional materials, and key areas for follow-up discussions in supervision.
    • View a webinar presentation from Sylvia Deporto (Assistant Director, Riverside County DPSS), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) providing an overview of these training tools for supervisors.
  • Supporting Youth to Successful Transitions from Foster Care: This curriculum provides an in-depth exploration of social work practice with non-minor dependent youth and includes key aspects of AB12 placement, benefits, and eligibility. It can be used in full or in separate modules to meet the needs of the agency and audience.
     
  • Extending Foster Care for Juvenile Justice Involved Youth: This curriculum provides and in-depth look at the benefits and policies related to extended foster care services for probation officers and others working with youth involved in the juvenile justice system.  It includes key aspects of transition jurisdiction, eligibility, placements, and benefits.  It can be used in full or in separate modules to meet the needs of the agency and audience.
     
  • Benefits Essentials: This brief curriculum provides an overview of the basic benefits for non-dependent minor youth, the benefits of extended Kin-GAP, and the benefits of extended AAP for youth ages 18 to 20.
    • View a webinar presentation from Debra Williams (CDSS), Paula Ensele (CDSS), Kathy Watkins (San Bernardino County), Angie Schwartz (Alliance for Children's Rights), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) with an overview of the Benefits Essentials Curriculum for trainers interested in using the curriculum.
       
  • After 18 and ICWA (Emerging Practice with Tribal Youth): This curriculum provides training on the intersection of After 18 and ICWA and includes specific tribal cultural considerations.  
  • Benefits for Eligibility Workers
    • View a webinar presentation from Ty Starks Morgan (CDSS), Teresa Arevalo (LA County Foster Care Division Chief), and Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants) providing information about extended foster care eligibility for eligibility workers.
    • Download the PowerPoint presentation.
       
  • After 18 Legal Process for Social Workers: This curriculum for social workers provides an overview of the four types of hearings that will occur for non-minor dependent youth and includes checklists for what to include in court reports for each hearing.
    • View a webinar presentation from Pat Bresee (Retired San Mateo County Commissioner), Lindsay Elliott (Children's Law Center of California), Kathy Watkins (San Bernardino County), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) with an overview of the Legal Process for Social Workers Curriculum for trainers interested in using the curriculum.
    • Access the 45-minute online training module Legal Process for Social Workers.
    • View a two-hour Legal Process Overview from the Adminstrative Office of the Courts.
  • Post-Secondary Education: Opportunities for Foster Youth in the Era of AB12: This curriculum was developed for social workers and probation officers to provide information about post-secondary education resources available for youth in extended foster care.
    • View a webinar presentation from Debbie Raucher (John Burton Foundation), Crystal Shackleford (Riverside County), Theresa Thurmond (CDSS), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) with an overview of the Post Secondary Education: Opportunities for Foster Youth in the Era of AB12 Curriculum for trainers interested in using the curriculum.
  • Guide to the Supervised Independent Living Placement (SILP) for Social Workers and Probation Officers: This curriculum walks trainees through the process of approving a SILP placement, including tips for readiness assessment and health and safety inspections.
    • View a webinar presentation from Simone Tureck (John Burton Foundation), Virginia Pierce (Monterey County), Karen Gunderson (CDSS), Lisa Molinar (Shared Vision Consultants), and Melissa Connelly (CalSWEC) with an overview of the Supervised Independent Living Placement Curriculum for trainers interested in using the curriculum.
  • Young Adults Transitioning from Group Care to Self-Sufficiency: This curriculum provides theoretical and practical information about best practices approaches to working with older adolescents and young adults transitioning from congregate care in the context of the newly developed program of extended foster care.
  • After 18 and Young Adults with Disabilities: This curriculum provides theoretical and practical information about best practices approaches to working with young adults with disabilities in the context of the newly developed program of extended foster care. 
  • Working with Caregivers to Support Non-Minor Dependent Youth in Extended Foster Care:  This curriculum provides tips and activities to build social worker's skills in supporting caregivers of transition age youth.
  • Services to Pregnant and Parenting Foster Youth: This curriculum is designed to provide social workers with knowledge and skills to help them provide services to pregnant and parenting young adults in extended foster care.
  • Best Practices for Engaging Eligible Youth in the CalFresh Program: This webinar provides an overview of the CalFresh program and gives some helpful tips for navigating the CalFresh application process with transitioning youth.  In addition, this supporting document from the National Center for Homeless Education provides tips on accessing additional food resources for youth.
