Student Recruitment and Selection

Warning message

You are currently visiting the archived CalSWEC website. To return to the new CalSWEC website at any time, go to https://calswec.berkeley.edu.

This section addresses how students are recruited and selected as stipend program recipients.

Program participants are recruited after they have been accepted as degree candidates by the schools of social work at their respective universities. Each university establishes its acceptance criteria based on the priority categories for admission noted below. Criteria may include motivation to serve the child welfare population and/or disenfranchised groups in California. 

  • Current staff members of county public social services agencies and employees of the California Department of Social Services (CDSS), who are qualified for admission to an MSW program
    • Current employees must obtain letters of support from their employer acknowledging the employer is aware of the application and supports it. Both full-time and part-time applicants must obtain such a letter. If educational leave or other forms of support are offered, these terms should be part of the letter.
  • Applicants who reflect the diverse client populations served by public child welfare in California
  • Other qualified individuals with demonstrated experience and commitment to careers providing high-quality, culturally sensitive practice in public child welfare services

Title IV-E Application Process

Full-Time students: To qualify for financial aid in the Title IV-E MSW/BASW program, Full-time students must first be admitted as a full-time student to a California graduate school of social work that participates in the IV-E program.Current employees of county social service departments are given admission preference, but they must be on educational leave (not receiving pay from the employer during the academic year) and have a letter of support from the county's director of child welfare services. The employee student may work during university break periods and summers and may perform fieldwork in their home agency subject to school and CSWE policies.

Stipend recipients must agree to participate in the complete child welfare specialization program as defined by the school, maintain a valid driver's license and secure use of a car for fieldwork, and undergo pre-screening for county employment. They must also fulfill a work commitment upon graduation.

Part-Time students: To qualify for financial aid in the Title IV-E MSW/BASW program, Part-time students must first be admitted as a part-time student to a California graduate school of social work that participates in the IV-E program. Admittance to part-time study is limited to current employees of county departments of social service, public child welfare services agencies, or the California Department of Social Services (CDSS). Applicants must have a letter of support from the director of the county's child welfare services agency. Part-time participants may perform fieldwork in their home agency subject to school and CSWE policies or do fieldwork in a qualified Community-Based Organization serving Title IV-E children and families.

Part-time participants must agree to participate in the complete child welfare specialization program as defined by the school, and maintain a valid driver's license and secure use of a car for fieldwork. They must also fulfill the work commitment upon graduation.

Forms and screening requirements:

Each applicant should complete a consent form for release of information to permit schools access to information relevant to a student's application. To be eligible, a prospective student must be a resident of California and a U.S. citizen or holder of a permanent resident visa. Students will also be asked about any criminal convictions and any prior participation in a IV-E program. A criminal background check will be required  for the student to enter field placement and/or employment in a child welfare services positions.

Interview process

Member universities are encouraged to require interviews for prospective IV-E recipients and prescribe sets of  interview questions to be asked of all applicants. Rating forms should be utilized to insure fairness and uniformity in the evaluation and stipend awards process. 

Awards process

Each school will develop an Awards Committee comprised of equal numbers of  agency and school representatives. All members of the Awards Committee will have an equal vote. Schools operating in the same geographical area may coordinate their awards processes.

Information about and applications for IV-E support should be available to all qualified admitted students as early as possible so that the awarding process will occur well before the beginning of fall classes. Completed applications must include proof of legal residency (a birth certificate, U.S. passport, or INS permanent residency form) and automobile (driver’s license, auto registration and insurance). All employees must provide a letter from the agency director as part of the application process, stating that said director is in support of their application. Letters of support will be taken into consideration in the awards process. There may be several levels of support, depending on the agency's capacity to offer support at that time. These levels include:

  • Supporting the employee’s admission, judges the employee capable of completing the academic program, and regards her/him as capable of MSW level work in the agency;
  • Retaining a position for the employee and agreeing, to the extent possible, to maintain benefits and seniority;
  • (For a full-time student) Providing the employee with a full-time educational leave; and
  • (For a part-time student) Making every effort to accommodate work expectations to the employee’s classroom and fieldwork requirements. This would include seeking to limit required work to three days a week while the employee is assigned to a field placement.

The Project Coordinator at each school will provide the Awards Committee with an orientation to the IV-E program and recruitment priorities and processes. The committee should establish factors it will use to select among high priority candidates if there are more such candidates than slots available. Such criteria might include:

  • Shortages of professional staff in particular counties;
  • Language skills particularly needed;
  • Amount of experience and academic promise;
  • Candidate’s evident commitment to the child welfare field.

The committee should also consider criteria to be used to select among non-priority applicants if slots remain after all priority candidates have been admitted. In no instance should a non-priority applicant be admitted before all high priority applicants have been accommodated, as this would violate the state contract.

Renewal of  financial support is not automatic. Your school’s Awards Committee should review the progress of first-year students prior to renewal of stipends for the second year. Students who fail to make satisfactory progress in the program in the first year should not receive an additional stipend until corrective action is taken. If a student is not in good standing and the Awards Committee chooses to continue that student’s funding, a justification letter for the decision must be sent to CalSWEC’s central office for approval.  Schools shall contract with students on a year-by-year basis, and shall retain the signed copy of the student contract and any other required documents.

Background Checks

Criminal background clearance must be completed on all students who receive stipends or tuition assistance. This may be accomplished in various ways.  Results must be consistent with the employment requirements of the county child welfare agencies likely to employ each school’s graduates. Before admission to the program, applicants must sign a statement (i.e., student contract) indicating that they have no relevant criminal history and affirming that any false information will result in denial of aid and/or repayment of any aid provided. After award, background checks may be accomplished in the most efficient manner for a particular school and county/counties. For example, a particular child welfare agency may be able to run the background checks, checks may be a part of the field placement process, or checks may be run through the county sheriff at minimal cost to students. In any case, the background check on every student must be completed within their first semester/quarter of enrollment in the IV-E program.  

Student Contracts and Other Documentation

Student and school shall sign the required student contract exhibited in the subcontract before any funds are awarded to the student. Copies of the required residency and transportation documents must be kept on file at each school. This documentation and the required materials for each student must be on file before the first quarterly reimbursement invoice can be processed.

SERVE: Indigenous Social Workers For Change (American Indian Recruitment Program)

SERVE (formerly American Indian Recruitment Program) supports the Title IV-E schools of social work in CalSWEC's consortium. Its goal is to increase the number of Native American social workers throughout the state and to fill a critical gap in the social work workforce. Eligible American Indian students can receive financial support as full- or part-time social work students with an emphasis on children and families in the Title IV-E Stipend Program.

SERVE is committed to full equity, justice, well-being, and cultural preservation of American Indian/Alaska Native children and families by

  • increasing the numbers of Indigenous social work graduates;
  • fostering inclusive leadership development;
  • developing partnerships between Tribal sovereign nations, indigenous communities, and other stakeholders; and
  • implementing decolonizing social work curricula to reflect community-based, culturally appropriate Indigenous values and the promotion of Indigenous sovereignty and self-determination.

SERVE has three designated project coordinators, two of whom are listed below, at universities in California's Northern, Central, and Southern regions, who work in collaboration with the Title IV-E Project Coordinators

  • Central region: Fresno State University

  • San Diego State University
    • Project Coordinator: Tamara Stroehauer
  • Humboldt State University
